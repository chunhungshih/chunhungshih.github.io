My name is Chun-Hung Shih. I'm currently a second-year PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Pennsylvania.

My research interests are syntax, syntax-semantics interface, and morphology. I'm particularly interested in argument structure, voice, case, and adverbial verbs, and I focus primarily on Formosan languages, Austronesian languages spoken in Taiwan. I'm doing fieldwork in Budai Ruakai, a variety of the Rukai language.

My advisor is Julie Anne Legate, and I also work with Florian Schwarz. I'm affiliated with the Penn Syntax Lab and the Experimental Study of Meaning Lab.

Before coming to UPenn, I completed my B.A. in Foreign Languages and Literatures and M.A. in linguisitics at the National Taiwan University, where I worked with Limay Sung on Budai Rukai.
