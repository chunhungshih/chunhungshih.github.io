My name is Chun-Hung Shih. I'm currently a second-year PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Pennsylvania.


My research interests are syntax, syntax-semantics interface, and morphology. I'm particularly interested in argument structure, voice, case, and adverbial verbs, and I focus primarily on Formosan languages, Austronesian languages spoken in Taiwan. I'm doing fieldwork mainly on Budai Ruakai and also on Katipul Puyuma. My advisor is Julie Anne Legate, and I also work with Florian Schwarz. I'm affiliated with the Penn Syntax Lab and the Experimental Study of Meaning Lab.


Before coming to UPenn, I completed my B.A. in Foreign Languages and Literatures and M.A. in Linguisitics at the National Taiwan University, where I started working on Budai Rukai with Li-May Sung.
