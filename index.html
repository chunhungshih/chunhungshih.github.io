Chun-Hung Shih

施俊宏 | [tɕyn hoŋ ʂɨ]

Hello!

My name is Chun-Hung Shih. I'm currently a second-year PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Pennsylvania.


My research interests are in syntax, the syntax-semantics interface, and morphology. I'm particularly interested in argument structure, voice, case, predicative possessives, and adverbial verbs, and I focus primarily on Formosan languages, Austronesian languages spoken in Taiwan. I'm doing fieldwork mainly on Budai Rukai and also on Katripul Puyuma. My advisor is Julie Anne Legate, and I also work with Florian Schwarz. I'm affiliated with the Penn Syntax Lab and the Penn Meaning Lab.


Before coming to UPenn, I completed my B.A. in Foreign Languages and Literatures and M.A. in Linguisitics at the National Taiwan University, where I started working on Budai Rukai under the guidance of Li-May Sung.


You can reach me at chshih@sas.upenn.edu.
